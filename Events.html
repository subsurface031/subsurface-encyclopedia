Upcoming Events

KwaZulu-Natal's coastal calendar is one of the richest in the southern hemisphere ‚Äî from the spectacular annual Sardine Run that transforms the South Coast into a feeding frenzy involving sharks, dolphins, and gannets, to the quiet magic of watching a loggerhead turtle lumber up a moonlit beach in Maputaland to lay her eggs. Throughout the year, the ocean brings extraordinary events to KZN's doorstep, and an informed coastal visitor is positioned to witness them at their best. This page tracks both the major public marine events and experiences available across KZN, and our own Sub-Surface events and guided experiences ‚Äî updated regularly as new dates and activities are confirmed.

Public events listed here are drawn from KZN's established annual marine calendar ‚Äî seasonal phenomena, guided ecotourism experiences, conservation volunteer days, and community events that bring people into meaningful contact with the ocean. Our own events ‚Äî beginning with the KZN Coastal Trek ‚Äî are designed to complement this calendar by offering a deeper, more immersive engagement with the coastal ecosystems profiled in this encyclopedia. The Sardine Run

üìç Wild Coast to South Coast KZN
üìÖ June ‚Äì July (peak: mid-June)
üéüÔ∏è Free to observe / guided dives available

The Sardine Run is one of the greatest wildlife spectacles on Earth ‚Äî billions of sardines migrating northward along the KZN South Coast in a mass so vast it can be seen from space. The sardines are followed by thousands of common and bottlenose dolphins, bronze whaler and dusky sharks, Cape gannets diving from above, and occasionally humpback whales and Bryde's whales that intercept the baitballs from below. Coastal towns like Port Shepstone, Margate, Scottburgh, and Amanzimtoti become focal points for spectators and divers during peak events, with operators offering snorkel and dive experiences within active baitballs. No two Sardine Run seasons are identical in timing or intensity ‚Äî but June is historically the most reliable window.

Humpback Whale Watching Season

üìç Entire KZN Coastline
üìÖ June ‚Äì November
üéüÔ∏è Boat-based tours from R350 ‚Äì R800pp

Over 30,000 humpback whales migrate through KZN's waters annually on one of the longest mammal migrations on the planet ‚Äî from their Antarctic feeding grounds northward to breeding and calving areas in the warm tropical Indian Ocean. KZN's warm coastal waters provide an ideal corridor, and whale watching from shore is possible at many headlands and elevated viewpoints along the coast from June through October. Boat-based whale watching tours operate from Durban, Ballito, Shelly Beach, and several other KZN ports, offering close-up encounters with breaching, spy-hopping, and tail-slapping whales at remarkably short distances. August and September typically offer the highest encounter rates as the southbound migration passes through.

Loggerhead & Leatherback Turtle Tours

üìç iSimangaliso Wetland Park ‚Äî Thonga Beach
üìÖ November ‚Äì January (guided nights)
üéüÔ∏è Ezemvelo-authorised guided tours only

The loggerhead and leatherback turtle nesting season on KZN's Maputaland beaches is one of Africa's most extraordinary wildlife experiences. Female turtles that hatched on these same beaches decades ago return every few years to lay their eggs, emerging from the darkness of the ocean to haul themselves above the high-tide line and excavate their nest chambers by hand. Ezemvelo KZN Wildlife authorises a limited number of guided night tours that allow visitors to observe nesting turtles under strict protocols ‚Äî no torches, no flash photography, minimum group sizes and maximum distances enforced. Accommodation at Bhanga Nek, Rocktail Beach Camp, and Thonga Beach Lodge provides the closest access to active nesting beaches.

African Penguin Colony ‚Äî Stony Point

üìç Betty's Bay, Western Cape (day trip from KZN)
üìÖ Year-round; peak July ‚Äì October (moult & breeding)
üéüÔ∏è Small entry fee

While African penguins do not breed in KZN itself, the Stony Point colony at Betty's Bay in the Western Cape ‚Äî one of only a few mainland colonies in the world ‚Äî is accessible as an extended drive or overnight trip for KZN visitors making their way around the coast. The colony is visible from a well-maintained boardwalk system that allows close observation without disturbance. Peak moult season (July ‚Äì September) sees the beach densely populated with moulting birds that cannot enter the water until their new feathers are fully grown. This is combined with breeding activity that makes the colony particularly active and engaging. Closer to KZN, occasional individual African penguins strand on KZN beaches ‚Äî any stranded or oiled penguin should be reported to SANCCOB's KZN response network.

KZN Beach & Estuary Clean-up Days

üìç Multiple KZN locations monthly
üìÖ Year-round ‚Äî major drives in Sep (Clean-up & Recycle) & Apr (Earth Month)
üéüÔ∏è Free ‚Äî volunteers welcome

Beach and estuary clean-up events are held regularly along KZN's coastline, coordinated by a range of organisations including the Two Oceans Aquarium Foundation, WILDOCEANS, Keep it Clean KZN, local surf clubs, and municipal environmental units. September's International Coastal Clean-up, coordinated globally by Ocean Conservancy and locally through various NGOs, is KZN's largest single-day beach clean-up event ‚Äî generating hundreds of volunteers across dozens of sites from Kosi Bay to the Wild Coast border simultaneously. Individual beach clean-ups are also run monthly at many locations by committed community groups. Participation requires no special equipment ‚Äî just your own bags and willingness to collect litter.

Manta Ray Season ‚Äî Sodwana Bay

üìç Sodwana Bay, iSimangaliso Wetland Park
üìÖ October ‚Äì February (peak: Nov ‚Äì Jan)
üéüÔ∏è Via registered dive operators only

Sodwana Bay's reef systems host seasonal aggregations of reef manta rays that arrive each year from October through February to feed on the plankton blooms generated by the area's productive inshore currents. Encounters with manta rays at Sodwana ‚Äî gliding effortlessly through the blue water above the reef, sometimes in groups of a dozen or more ‚Äî are among the most memorable wildlife experiences available to divers anywhere in southern Africa. Several Sodwana dive operators run dedicated manta-focused dive excursions during peak season. Mantas can also be encountered while snorkelling at certain sites during periods of particularly dense plankton blooms in the surface water.

Dolphin Watching & Swim-with-Dolphins

üìç Durban, Umhlanga, Sodwana, Shelly Beach
üìÖ Year-round; peak encounters during Sardine Run
üéüÔ∏è Boat tours from R350pp

Bottlenose and common dolphins are year-round residents of KZN's inshore waters, frequently encountered from shore and almost guaranteed on any boat-based excursion. The Sardine Run months bring tens of thousands of common dolphins to the South Coast in the largest marine mammal aggregations visible from land anywhere in Africa. During quieter months, resident Durban bottlenose dolphins are reliably encountered close inshore, often bow-riding alongside vessels. Responsible swim-with-dolphin experiences operate from several KZN ports ‚Äî prioritise operators who follow DFFE guidelines on approach distances and interaction protocols, and who do not harass animals for the benefit of photographs.

Sodwana Bay Open Diving Season

üìç Sodwana Bay ‚Äî Two Mile to Nine Mile Reef
üìÖ Year-round; peak visibility Oct ‚Äì May
üéüÔ∏è Dive permits required ‚Äî via SANParks

Sodwana Bay is South Africa's premier coral reef diving destination and one of the top reef dive sites in the Indian Ocean. The reef system extends from Two Mile Reef ‚Äî a shallow, current-swept wall hosting enormous schools of reef fish ‚Äî through to the more remote Nine Mile and Quarter Mile reefs with their pristine hard coral gardens. The site is accessible to divers of all experience levels, from open-water beginners at sheltered launch sites to advanced divers exploring the deeper walls. Resident species include potato bass, ragged-tooth sharks (winter months), loggerhead turtles, moray eels, and occasionally whale sharks. Dive entry requires SANParks permits obtained in advance.

World Ocean Day & Marine Conservation Events

üìç Durban & various KZN locations
üìÖ June 8 (World Ocean Day) & September (Ocean Month)
üéüÔ∏è Mostly free ‚Äî check organisers for details

World Ocean Day on 8 June and South Africa's Ocean Month in September anchor a series of marine conservation public events across KZN each year. These range from school education programmes and public talks hosted by uShaka Marine World and the eThekwini Beach Safety Unit, to shoreline and estuary monitoring citizen science days run by academic institutions and NGOs, to community awareness campaigns in coastal informal settlements most affected by marine pollution and estuary degradation. Events are coordinated across multiple organisations and vary year to year ‚Äî follow the WILDOCEANS and Two Oceans Aquarium Foundation social media channels for current listings as the dates approach.

Stay Connected ‚Äî Never Miss an Event

KZN's marine events calendar is dynamic ‚Äî the timing of natural phenomena like the Sardine Run and humpback whale migration varies year to year depending on ocean temperatures, currents, and prey availability. The best way to stay informed on current conditions and event timing is to follow organisations and community groups with eyes on the water. For the Sardine Run specifically, the Sardine Run website and community groups including Shaka Barker Tours and Salt Rock Sharks provide up-to-the-day baitball and dolphin reports during the June ‚Äì July window.
